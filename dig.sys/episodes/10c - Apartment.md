# 10c - Apartment
**POV: Becky**

---

Dust motes drift in columns of light that cut through the cracked blinds. The light divides the floor into yellow and grey stripes. Three mugs sit on the small kitchen table, each one with a brown ring drying at the bottom. The table is a stripped expanse of laminate, cleared down to almost nothing. There are no coasters, no stray mail, no salt shakers. My blanket is a heap on the couch, a mass of grey wool that holds the indentation of where I spent the night.

Alex is standing by the window. She is not looking out through the blinds. She is standing with her shoulders slightly hunched, her weight shifted to her left leg. She hasn't had coffee. She has not moved from that spot in ten minutes.

The black case of Glitter sits on the counter, in the place it normally sits. The matte finish of the plastic absorbs the morning light. The case is closed, hiding the small glass vials inside. It is the only object on the counter without a layer of dust.

The door opens. Roni walks in. She has a yellow legal pad tucked under her arm. Dark circles ring her eyes. She walks to the table, sets the pad down on the laminate, and looks at Alex. Roni does not speak. She opens the pad to a fresh page and holds a pen in her right hand.

Alex does not move.

A few minutes later, Diana comes through the door. She carries her tablet, the screen dimmed to a low glow. Her hair is matted at the back. There is a smudge of dark grey grease on her forearm, just below the elbow. She does not greet anyone. She slides into a chair at the table and taps the screen with a slow, rhythmic motion of her index finger.

The knock at the door is soft. Carla walks in carrying a brown paper bag of bagels from the clinic's breakroom. She does not ask to come in. She walks to the counter and sets the food down right next to the black case of Glitter. She looks at the group: the slumped shoulders, the empty mugs, the way we are spaced across the room.

Carla does not offer a hug. She does not ask how we are. She looks at the legal pad on the table and then looks at us.

"You're playing a game where the house already won," Carla says.

She turns and walks out. The door clicks shut. The smell of toasted sesame and cream cheese fills the space.

The door opens again. Sam enters. They do not make a sound. They lean their shoulder against the frame and watch the room. Their eyes move from Roni to Diana, then to Alex. They stay there, leaning, without speaking.

Beth is sitting on the corner of the couch. Her spine is curved. Her eyes are fixed on a point on the floor, about six inches in front of her feet. She is sitting up, but her shoulders are slumped forward.

"I can do this today," Beth says. Her voice is a dry whisper. "I'm here."

Roni does not look at Beth. She opens the legal pad and points to the top of the page.

"Audit," Roni says. "Day 4."

Roni looks at the column for finances.

"Money on hand," Roni continues. "We got nothing from the tour. $0. We have the two hundred baseline funds. That's all we have."

Diana does not look up from her tablet. The blue light of the screen reflects in her pupils. "I spent the night on the distribution nodes," she says. Her voice is flat. "I mapped the secondary drop points for Jaw-man's mid-level guys. There are gaps. Small ones. If we can wedge ourselves into the overflow, we can start scraping."

Roni looks at the tablet. "How long?"

"Weeks," Diana says. "Maybe a month to get a foothold. It's a slow burn. It won't get us ten thousand in twenty-six days."

The refrigerator hums. Roni taps the pen against the legal pad.

"We can't wait for gaps in the supply chain," Roni says. "We can't scrape for weeks. We don't have the time."

Roni leans forward, placing her elbows on the table.

"We stop looking for a side door," Roni says. "We run actual product. We cut Jaw-man's supply chain and sell on our own corner. We take the risk and we take the profit."

I look at Beth. She does not move. I look at Diana. She has stopped tapping the tablet. I look at Sam. They are still leaning against the doorframe.

Then I look at Alex.

Alex is not looking at Roni. She is not looking at any of us.

She is looking at the black case of Glitter on the counter.

She is perfectly still. Her head is tilted a fraction of a degree. Her eyes are fixed on the center of the matte black lid. She does not blink. Ten seconds pass. Then twenty. Her pupils are wide, catching the light from the blinds.

"We can't do that," Beth says. Her voice is barely audible. "Jaw-man will kill us."

"He might," Roni says. "But the clock is already killing us."

"Let's sleep on it," Diana says. "Give me another night to see if I can find a faster way."

Roni does not answer. She closes the legal pad with a sharp snap of the paper.

Diana stands up and walks toward the door. Sam follows her, stepping away from the frame. Roni gathers her pen and the legal pad and leaves without a word.

Beth stays for a moment, then stands and moves toward the bedroom. She does not look at Alex.

I stay on the couch. I watch Alex.

She is still standing by the window. The light has shifted, moving a few inches across the floor. The room is empty except for the two of us and the smell of bagels.

Alex has not moved. Her hand is curled around the pendant at her neck. Her thumb rubs the metal in a slow, rhythmic circle. She is still looking at the black case on the counter.

The light hits the case, casting a long shadow across the laminate. Alex keeps her eyes on the plastic.
