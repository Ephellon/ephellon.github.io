
# 12c — Open Latch
<strong class="meta-line">POV  &bull;  Becky</strong>

---

The apartment smells of old sesame and the faint, metallic tang of the radiator. Warm yellow bars of sunlight stretch across the floor, shifting slow. 

Beth’s bag sits by the door. Slumped. The leather strap hasn't moved since 
yesterday. Nobody looks at it. Roni doesn't mention it. Diana doesn't mention it.
I stop by the counter. The black Glitter case is there. The latch is up. 

I look at the vials. The foam inside is undisturbed. Alex is standing right there. She isn't at the window today. She’s at the counter, her hand resting on the laminate an inch from the case. She doesn't look at me.

Roni leans back in her chair, her legal pad open on the table. She doesn't 
look at the door. She doesn't look for Beth.

"We stop looking for a side door," Roni says. "We run actual product."

The room stays quiet. Alex doesn't move.

"The lot behind the old cinema on 4th," Roni continues. "Five vials. That’s the foothold. We move them fast and we use the cash to clear the first tier of the clock."

Diana shifts her weight. She doesn't meet Roni's eyes. 

"I could try another scrape," Diana says. "Different traffic patterns on the local hubs. It might buy us a few more days if the signal is clean. We 
might find a cleaner way in without the physical hand-off."

Roni doesn't blink. She just lets the proposal sit there, naming the corner and the quantity.

I look at Sam. Sam is staring into a half-empty coffee mug. He doesn't say 
a word. He doesn't nod or shake his head. He just keeps his shoulders hunched, his thumb tracing the rim of the ceramic over and over. 

Alex is still. Her hand is hovering, the fingers slightly curled, not touching the case but not pulling away. She hasn't spoken since I walked in. She doesn't look at Roni. She just watches the light hit the open latch of the case.

The radiator hisses. A thin strip of yellow sun slides off the counter and 
onto the floor.

Alex’s index finger moves. She touches the metal of the latch. She presses 
down.

The latch clicks shut.
