# Episode 0 — Session Zero

The bootstrap session. PCs spawn into the world before Alex's eviction, acquire their starting gear in fiction, and surface the bond to Alex that brings them into her orbit when Ep 1 begins. No clock yet. No debt yet. The trade has not started for any of them.

This document is GM-facing. It is the procedural guide for running Session 0 at the table. It is not Becky-narrated prose.

---

## What Session 0 does

Three things, in order:

1. **Establishes each PC's pre-trade life.** Where they live, what their day looks like before Alex's call. The "before."
2. **Hands each PC their playbook gear in the fiction.** Each item is acquired through an action — a trip to a workshop, a stop at a stash, a meeting with someone who owes them. No item appears in inventory without a scene attached.
3. **Surfaces each PC's bond to Alex.** The bond was written at character creation. Session 0 brings it into play — through a memory, a short scene, an action.

Session 0 closes when every PC has gear, has a bond surfaced, and is in position for Ep 1 to land. The phone call from Alex — or the news of her eviction reaching them — is the closing beat.

---

## What the GM does before the table

1. **Confirm every PC's playbook.** Each PC has chosen one of the 12 archetypes (Tinkerer / Roughneck / Voice / Faithful / Wired / Vet / Clean / Believer / Outsider / Grifter / Touched / Reader). Each playbook lists the 5 starting items.
2. **Confirm every PC's bond prompt.** Each playbook has a bond prompt asking *why* — a specific narrative hook to Alex. Each PC has answered it during character creation.
3. **Establish each PC's "home" in fiction.** A location in or around Blueville they consider home — a house, an apartment, a trailer, a cabin, a back room. The GM should know each one before the table sits down, or ask the player to define it at the top of the session.
4. **Read Ep 1 in advance.** Session 0 closes at the threshold of Ep 1; the GM needs to know what is about to land.

---

## Opening the session — sample read-aloud

The GM reads aloud, or paraphrases. Sets the time-just-before:

> Blueville in late spring, before everything went sideways. Storm season hasn't started yet. The heat is the kind that settles in by ten in the morning and doesn't leave until after dark. The president's hometown signs are sun-bleached. Pilgrims are starting to trickle in for the summer. Most people in Blueville peaked in high school and are still wearing the proof.
>
> You all live here. You all know each other, in the way people in a town of this size know each other — by face, by name, by which side of which dispute your families are on. And you all know Alex.
>
> That's where this story starts. Not yet — but soon.

After the read, go around the table. Ask each player: *Where are you right now? It's a regular day. What does it look like?*

Each PC describes their "home" and what they're doing in it. No rolls. This is establishment.

## The gear acquisition sequence

For each PC, in any table order:

### Step 1 — Frame the trip

The PC needs their gear. The GM asks: *Where is your gear, and why aren't you carrying it right now?*

Each PC has 5 playbook items. The fiction must explain where each one lives. Some answers are obvious (the wallet is in the back pocket; the phone is in hand). Some are not (the vehicle is at the garage; the sidearm is at the storage unit; the soldering iron is at the workshop). Anything not on the PC's person needs a location.

The PC then describes the trip to gather their gear. A vehicle ride to an abandoned warehouse. A walk from the lake house to the Gentleman's club. A stop at a friend's garage. A visit to a self-storage facility. The trip is narrated, not rolled — unless something in fiction triggers a basic move. Move Product does not apply yet. Talk Fast or Read a Person might if the trip involves another character.

### Step 2 — Roll for condition

Each item gets a **1d6 condition roll** as the PC takes possession of it.

| Roll | Condition | Description |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Failing** | Close to broken. Visible decay. Works, but the GM can call its failure at a dramatic moment without warning. |
| 2–3 | **Worn** | Used hard, used often. Functional. The GM can introduce complications around it under stress (a misfire, a jam, a dropped call). |
| 4–5 | **Functional** | Baseline working. No mechanical effect. The default state of useful gear. |
| 6 | **Fresh** | Better than baseline. Well-kept, well-loved, lucky. Once per session, the PC can spend the gear's freshness for **+1 forward** on a move that meaningfully involves it. After the spend, the item drops to Functional. |

The PC narrates the condition into the fiction as they take possession. *"The pistol's been in the warehouse for two years. I roll a 4 — Functional. Still oiled. Good."* Or: *"I roll a 2 on the clothes. Worn. They've been folded in a garbage bag too long. They smell like the bag."*

The condition is canon for the PC's playthrough. It can change in play — a Tinkerer can restore a Failing item; a fight can knock a Functional item to Worn.

### Step 3 — Surface the bond

After gear acquisition, the GM asks each PC: *You wrote a bond to Alex at character creation. When was the last time you saw her, or talked to her, or thought about her? What was that like?*

The PC answers in fiction. One short scene, one short memory, one short observation. No roll. The bond stays at +1, but the relationship now exists at the table — same number, different weight.

## Worked examples — Kevin (Vet) and Luigi (Tinkerer)

These were the first two PCs at the table for the original Session 0 run. They are canon.

### Kevin

> **Home.** A small house on the east side of town. Kept tidy. The Ford Ranger lives in the driveway. Kevin walks out to it at the start of the session.

> **Vehicle — Ford Ranger** (Vet's "a vehicle that runs"). *Roll: 2.* Worn. Runs, but not well enough to make it to the next town over. Kevin notes this in fiction; the truck is a Blueville-only vehicle now.

> **Trip.** Kevin drives the Ranger to an abandoned warehouse on the east side. He used to use it as a private range. His remaining gear is locked in a footlocker there.

> **Sidearm — conventional pistol** (Vet's "a sidearm, laser or conventional, your pick"). *Roll: 4.* Functional. Oiled, loaded, no story.

> **Folding knife** (Vet's "a folding knife you've sharpened too many times"). *Roll: 1.* Failing. The blade is short from sharpening. Kevin keeps it anyway.

> **Bag with three days of clothes** (Vet's "a bag with three days of clothes always packed"). *Roll: 1.* Failing. Tattered. Folded in the bag since the last time he packed. Some have holes. They smell wrong.

> **Challenge coin** (Vet's "challenge coin or equivalent token from the unit/job/place"). *Roll: 6.* Fresh. Polished, displayed in a small velvet bag inside the footlocker. Kevin can spend the coin's freshness once per session for +1 forward on a move where the coin meaningfully figures.

> **Bond to Alex.** Kevin watched Alex grow up at a distance, and came back from somewhere recently to find she was suddenly an adult. The last time he saw her: a grocery store run-in, three weeks ago. She was buying frozen peas. She nodded. He nodded back. That was the conversation.

### Luigi

> **Home.** A "lake house" — a one-room cabin on the edge of one of the climate-stressed inland reservoirs. Kept livable, barely. Luigi wakes up and walks out.

> **Trip.** Luigi walks from the lake house to **the Gentleman's club**, where his workshop space is. The specific nature of the Gentleman's club is canon-open — strip club, actual gentleman's social club, gym, lodge. The GM and player together establish what kind of place it is, the first time it figures in play.

> **Multi-tool** (Tinkerer's "multi-tool, well-worn"). *Roll: 3.* Worn. Functional, used. The roll matches the playbook flavor exactly.

> **Tablet running three OSes** (Tinkerer's "tablet, lifted or salvaged, runs three OSes"). *Roll: 2.* Worn. One of the three OSes is acting weird. Luigi is not sure how to fix it. The Tinkerer's Jury-Rig and Schematic Sight moves apply when he tries.

> **Cargo pants** (Tinkerer's "cargo pants with too many pockets"). *Roll: 5.* Functional. All pockets intact, nothing torn.

> **Soldering iron** (Tinkerer's "soldering iron, 2050s spec"). *Roll: 4.* Functional. Still heats. Still works.

> **Busted phone full of harvested parts** (Tinkerer's fifth item). *Not yet established.* Luigi did not retrieve this item during the original Session 0. When Luigi next has a beat with this phone, the GM calls the condition roll on the spot. Until rolled, the item is canonically unestablished.

> **Bond to Alex.** Luigi set Alex up with something digital once — a phone, an account, a connection. The specifics surface when Luigi's bond enters play.

## Closing Session 0

When every PC has gear, condition, and bond surfaced:

1. **The phone rings, or the news arrives.** Alex calls one of them. Or word reaches them — Alex is out of her mom's place. Alex needs somewhere to go. Alex is at the Smoke Shop. Whatever fits the GM's pacing.
2. **The PCs respond.** No rolls. They simply respond. Some go to her. Some make calls. Some sit with the news. Whatever they do, the response is the bridge to Episode 1.
3. **End the session.** Tell the players the next session opens with Ep 1 — Alex's eviction, the inciting beat, the day everything tips.

## Notes for v0.2

- **Other PCs' Session 0 sequences** canonize as they play. Each PC's home, gear locations, condition rolls, and bond surfacing become canon for their character.
- **The 1d6 condition mechanic** is calibrated for texture, not load-bearing math. If play reveals the curve is wrong (too many Failing items, not enough Fresh ones), adjust the table.
- **Bond confirmation** is currently asked in narration. If a more structured prompt list would help GMs run this consistently, codify in v0.2.
- **The "Gentleman's club"** as a specific location — canonize once Luigi's player establishes its nature in play.
