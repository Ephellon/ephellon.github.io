# Digital Sissy — Playbooks

The 12 archetypes. Players pick from these at character creation; unpicked playbooks become NPCs in the fiction.

Each playbook contains: a pitch, a look, a stat distribution, three starting moves (signature + two more), four advancement moves (chosen one at a time on advancement), starting gear, a bond template, and an end-of-session XP trigger.

Companion to `Digital Sissy (PbtA System Spec).md`. Mechanical conventions (2d6+stat, the four tracks, basic moves) live in that doc.

---

## How to Read a Playbook

**Pitch** — the in-fiction identity. The kind of person this is in Blueville.

**Look** — the silhouette. Not a specific person; the way this archetype tends to read.

**Stats** — the playbook's distribution of the standard array (+2 / +1 / +1 / 0 / -1).

**Starting Moves** — the three moves every PC of this playbook has from session one. The first is the signature.

**Advancement Moves** — picked one at a time when a PC advances. v0.1 lists four per playbook; more will be added in v0.2 as advancement gets stress-tested in play.

**Gear** — what this PC starts with. The fiction can establish more, but these are baseline.

**Bond** — every PC starts with **+1 bond to Alex** (canon). The bond prompt here is what the PC fills in during character creation to describe *why*. Bonds to other PCs are filled in at the table.

**XP Trigger** — the archetype-specific thing this PC does to earn XP, on top of the base triggers (missed roll, acted on a bond, took an archetype-fitting risk).

## The Tinkerer
*Source: Artificer*

**Pitch.** Auto-shop type. Lives in his garage. Makes broken things work because nobody's coming to fix them — not the landlord, not the city, not the manufacturer who sold the thing in the first place. Has a Plex server he built himself and is irrationally proud of it.

**Look.** Oil-stained hands he can't fully wash. Cargo pants, three pens in one pocket. Reads tools the way other people read faces. Hat optional but probable.

**Stats.** +2 Slick / +1 Grit / +1 Heart / 0 Cool / -1 Charm

### Starting Moves

**Jury-Rig** (signature) — When you spend at least a scene alone with a busted thing, roll +Slick. On 10+, you make it work for the rest of the session. On 7–9, it works once when you need it, then breaks worse. On 6–, it breaks worse without working.

**Salvage** — When you spend time pulling apart broken hardware, you can recover one useful component without spending cash. State its purpose; the GM honors it within reason.

**The Workshop** — Your garage is yours. Anyone you bring there in a scene of presence heals 1 Wear. The Workshop's location is fictional canon; the GM may threaten it but not casually take it.

### Advancement Moves

**Schematic Sight** — When you study a machine, system, or built thing, roll +Slick. On 10+, the GM tells you how it could be turned against itself or its owner. On 7–9, you see the obvious vulnerability.

**Field Modify** — Once per session, give a piece of gear a temporary new function for one scene. State the function; the GM honors it if the fiction can stretch.

**The Rig** — Your personal device (phone, tool, vehicle, your pick) has three custom functions you describe; they work as you say. If lost or destroyed, you can rebuild it over one session.

**Run It Hot** — When you push a machine past spec, roll +Slick. On 10+, it does the extra thing and survives. On 7–9, it does the thing and breaks. On 6–, it breaks before doing the thing, in a way that costs.

### Gear

- Multitool, well-worn
- Tablet, lifted or salvaged, runs three OSes
- Cargo pants with too many pockets
- Soldering iron, 2050s spec (wireless, runs hot enough to burn)
- A busted phone full of harvested parts

### Bond

You fixed something of Alex's once that mattered to her. Describe what, and what she still owes you for it (cash, silence, a favor, knowledge).

### XP Trigger

Mark XP when you fix something irreplaceable to someone.

## The Roughneck
*Source: Barbarian*

**Pitch.** Felt everything as a physical fact since middle school. Hits before words because words never worked at home. Knows three bouncers by name. Got kicked off the football team for one fight too many; never quite recovered the social position.

**Look.** Heavy frame whether muscled or just thick. Knuckles permanently red. Wears the same jacket year-round. Stands like someone who's already calculated which way to swing.

**Stats.** +2 Grit / +1 Cool / +1 Heart / 0 Slick / -1 Charm

### Starting Moves

**Take It For Them** (signature) — When you put yourself between violence and another character, mark the harm on whichever of your tracks (Body or Wear) the GM picks — the one that hurts worse for you right now.

**Pound for Pound** — When you initiate violence first in an exchange, every harm in that exchange comes in at one ladder rung lower than the GM would otherwise assign (1-body instead of 2, 2 instead of 3). The discount runs out when you stop initiating.

**The Old Crowd** — You know other Roughnecks across Blueville — guys you fought with, guys you fought against, guys whose brothers you know. Once per session, summon one to the scene. They show up. They want something.

### Advancement Moves

**The Helmet** — Like Jaw-man, you took a hit to the head once. When you remember the moment, mark Body harm and gain +1 Cool until session end. Once per session.

**Bruiser** — Your fists count as armed for harm purposes. The fiction reflects this; people remember the last fight before they pick the next one.

**Read the Body** — When you watch someone for a moment, you can tell what they've been through physically — recent injuries, missed sleep, hunger, fear. The GM tells you one thing.

**Won't Drop** — When you would mark dying on the Body track, you can instead mark 3-body twice. You're useless for the rest of the scene but you're not dying. Once per session.

### Gear

- Old varsity jacket (Blueville High, two seasons)
- Folding knife
- Flask
- Brass knuckles or equivalent (a roll of quarters in a sock works)
- A key to somewhere you shouldn't have a key to

### Bond

You hit somebody for Alex once. Describe when, who, and whether either of you ever spoke about it after.

### XP Trigger

Mark XP when you take harm meant for someone you cared about.

## The Voice
*Source: Bard*

**Pitch.** Karaoke regular who almost had a YouTube moment in high school. Streams to 80 followers, half of them bots. Knows every song at every bar in Blueville and the right time to call each one. Can make a smoke session feel like a party even when nobody's having fun.

**Look.** Dressed slightly above the room. Voice already a little gravelly from use. Touches their throat when they're nervous. Always knows where the light is.

**Stats.** +2 Charm / +1 Heart / +1 Slick / 0 Cool / -1 Grit

### Starting Moves

**Work The Room** (signature) — When you perform for a group of three or more, roll +Charm. On 10+, everyone present takes -1 forward to oppose you for the rest of the scene. On 7–9, choose two of the room. On 6–, you become the center of unwanted attention.

**The Gig** — Once per session, declare you have a gig — karaoke night, a party, a wedding, a livestream, an open mic. Establish the venue. While there, you have +1 to Charm rolls.

**Pitch Perfect** — When you echo someone's exact words back to them in the same tone and rhythm, roll +Charm. On 10+, they go along with whatever the echo implied. On 7–9, they go along but notice. On 6–, they recognize the tactic.

### Advancement Moves

**Followers** — Name your small online audience. Once per session, leverage them for an information request, a small favor, or a piece of public attention. The audience does not show up in person.

**The Hook** — When you slip an earworm phrase into a conversation, the target uses it back to you within the same scene. You can use that moment for +1 forward to a social move.

**Cover Song** — When you briefly take on someone else's identity in front of someone who knows them, roll +Charm to be convincing. On 10+, full pass. On 7–9, pass with a tell. On 6–, they know.

**Stage Mom Energy** — When you publicly advocate hard for another PC in a scene, that PC takes +1 forward to their next move.

### Gear

- Phone with a real microphone setup attached
- A change of clothes for the venue
- Drink ticket book or equivalent (always has a way in)
- Earpiece, tiny, half-modded
- A signed setlist from the one show that mattered

### Bond

You and Alex used to sing together (or you sang and she watched, or she sang and you watched). Describe the song and the night.

### XP Trigger

Mark XP when you perform publicly in a way that mattered to the outcome of a scene.

## The Faithful
*Source: Cleric*

**Pitch.** Pentecostal grandma's grandkid. Hospice aide or church pantry volunteer or both. Shows up when nobody else does. Gives away what she can't spare. Knows the right hymn for the right moment but doesn't always sing it.

**Look.** Tired-eyed. Sensible shoes. Dressed plainly. Hands that have washed a lot of dishes and held a lot of other hands. Carries something small that smells faintly of something — coffee, soap, lavender, somebody's perfume.

**Stats.** +2 Heart / +1 Charm / +1 Grit / 0 Cool / -1 Slick

### Starting Moves

**Tend** (signature) — When you stay with someone in their hurt for a full scene, they heal 1 harm (Body or Wear, their pick), and you take 1 Wear.

**Witness Bearing** — When you tell the truth at cost to yourself, mark 1 XP immediately and gain +1 forward on your next Charm roll.

**The Pantry** — You keep a small store of food, supplies, or cash you intend to give away. Once per session, give 1 unit of help to anyone — heal 1 Wear, shelter for a night, hide them for a scene.

### Advancement Moves

**Laying On Hands** — When you Tend, you can heal 2 harm instead of 1. You take 2 Wear instead of 1.

**The Congregation** — Name your church, mutual aid group, or community network. Once per session, call on them collectively — alibi, search party, prayer chain that produces actual leads, a meal for ten.

**Vigil** — When you sit with someone in the dying state, they cannot reach dead until you leave the room. You take 1 Wear at the start of each scene of vigil.

**Forgiveness** — When you forgive someone genuinely and they know it, reset your bond to them to base value. They reduce a track of your choice by 1 step.

### Gear

- A Bible or equivalent, well-used
- A casserole dish (always either full or being returned)
- Small bills, kept folded
- Phone full of people's birthdays and prescription schedules
- A blanket that's been washed a thousand times

### Bond

You knew Alex's mom (Cindy) before Alex was born, or you knew Alex before her mom got mean. Describe the version of her family you remember.

### XP Trigger

Mark XP when you give something away that you couldn't spare.

## The Wired
*Source: Druid (techno-nerd)*

**Pitch.** Talks to networks the way some people talk to dogs. Server-room kid grown up. Doesn't disconnect even when she sleeps. Notices when the WiFi is wrong before anyone else does. Lives behind three monitors and a habit of speaking to her devices by name.

**Look.** Pale from the screens. Wired earpiece always in. Backpack with three battery packs visible. Phone in hand even mid-conversation. Eyes flick to notifications she alone can see.

**Stats.** +2 Heart / +1 Slick / +1 Cool / 0 Charm / -1 Grit

### Starting Moves

**Commune** (signature) — When you spend time alone with a connected device, roll +Heart. On 10+, ask 2 from the list: who's been here, what they wanted, who they spoke to, what they hid, where they went after. On 7–9, ask 1. On 6–, the system pushes back — the GM tells you what notices you.

**Always Online** — Given a scene of access, you can attempt to find any digital information. Roll +Heart. On 10+, you find it cleanly. On 7–9, you find it but trip an alarm. On 6–, you find a counterfeit and don't know it.

**Distributed** — You have backups everywhere. If a device of yours is taken, destroyed, or compromised, you can recover its contents within one session from a different node.

### Advancement Moves

**Push** — Once per session, push a connected device to do something it shouldn't — open a door it controls, log a fake entry, ping a target's location. The GM tells you what the system can stretch to.

**Wraith** — When you would be tracked digitally, you aren't. State why your trail goes cold; the fiction accommodates.

**Pet** — Name a personal device with minor agency — a drone, a modded phone, a bot. It obeys you within reason and has its own small character.

**Hivemind** — When you tap into a network of devices in an area (a building, a block, a venue), ask the GM 1 question about anyone present.

### Gear

- Phone (modded, custom OS)
- Tablet (also modded)
- Battery banks, plural
- A drone or similar, small enough to carry
- A device only you understand

### Bond

You set Alex up with something digital once — a phone, an account, a connection. You know what it's named on. Describe what.

### XP Trigger

Mark XP when you used technology to know something no one else could.

## The Vet
*Source: Fighter*

**Pitch.** Discharged early, or honorably, or somewhere in between. Knows how to hurt people because someone taught them, which is different from the Roughneck. The discipline outlasted the uniform. Works odd jobs and watches the door.

**Look.** Holds posture even when relaxed. Eyes scan the exits when they walk in. Hair shorter than fashion. Hands quiet. Doesn't startle.

**Stats.** +2 Cool / +1 Grit / +1 Slick / 0 Heart / -1 Charm

### Starting Moves

**Read The Fight** (signature) — When violence is about to start, roll +Cool. On 10+, name the outcome before the first blow lands; the GM honors it within reason. On 7–9, you see one move ahead. On 6–, you freeze for the first beat.

**First Move** — When you act first in a violent exchange, roll +Cool. On 10+, choose two: you hit, you avoid being hit, you reposition advantageously, you disarm them. On 7–9, choose one.

**The Drill** — You have a routine that calms you. When you take a moment to drill — push-ups, breath count, weapons check — clear 1 Wear. Once per session.

### Advancement Moves

**Old Wound** — Your old injury aches before violence. When you notice the ache, gain +1 forward on Cool for the encounter. The injury sometimes lies.

**Suppress** — Make someone keep their head down for a scene. On 10+, they don't act until the scene shifts. On 7–9, they don't act until you give them an opening or look away.

**The Buddy** — Name another vet you know in Blueville. They show up once per session for one ask. They're more reluctant each time.

**Trained Eye** — When you assess a violent situation, the GM tells you the weak link — the person, the object, the angle that's going to break first.

### Gear

- A sidearm (laser or conventional, your pick — the canon allows laser as accent)
- A folding knife you've sharpened too many times
- A vehicle that runs (truck, motorcycle, beater sedan)
- A bag with three days of clothes always packed
- A challenge coin or equivalent token from the unit/job/place

### Bond

You watched Alex grow up at a distance, or you came back from somewhere and she was suddenly an adult. Describe what changed about her between the times you saw her.

### XP Trigger

Mark XP when you saw the violence coming and acted on it.

## The Clean
*Source: Monk*

**Pitch.** Six months sober. Twelve months. Eighteen days again. Knows how easy the slip is, knows the shape of the pull, knows which words the people who love them stopped saying. Sponsors others to keep their own discipline taut.

**Look.** Lean. Carries less than they used to. Wears one good outfit and washes it. Coffee always in hand. Speaks more carefully than people expect, with longer pauses than feel comfortable.

**Stats.** +2 Cool / +1 Grit / +1 Heart / 0 Slick / -1 Charm

### Starting Moves

**Sponsor** (signature) — When you carry someone through their withdrawal, breaking, or worst moment in a full scene of presence, reduce their using track or wear track by 1 step. You take 1 Wear and gain +1 forward on your next Hold the Line.

**The Step** — When you're tempted to use, dose, fight, lie, or cheat and you don't, mark XP. Once per scene.

**One Day At A Time** — At session start, declare today's intention in one sentence. If you uphold it through the session, gain +1 forward on Cool to the first roll of next session.

### Advancement Moves

**The Meeting** — Name a recovery meeting in Blueville (NA, AA, a Pentecostal group, an online circle). You can access it once per session for support, anonymity, or word-of-mouth information.

**Sit With It** — When you experience a hard emotion fully without acting on it, mark XP and clear 1 Wear. Once per scene.

**Center** — Once per session, before a roll, take a breath. Reroll one die.

**Higher Power** — Name what you turned your life over to. Once per session, invoke it for guidance; the GM gives you a one-word answer.

### Gear

- A coffee thermos (refilled, never emptied)
- A token, chip, or note marking time
- A small phonebook of sponsees and sponsors
- A bag of supplies for somebody else's worst night
- Reading material that helps (Big Book, a Bible, a worn paperback)

### Bond

You knew Alex when one of you was using and the other wasn't. Describe which way it was, and whether the other one knew at the time.

### XP Trigger

Mark XP when you resisted the pull.

## The Believer
*Source: Paladin*

**Pitch.** Has a code. The code is the code. Could be religious, political, personal, learned-the-hard-way — anchored to one principle and not negotiating. Sometimes wrong about what's right. Never wrong about whether to act on it.

**Look.** Stands straight without trying. Eyes that don't drift in arguments. Wears a single symbol of the principle, somewhere obvious — a cross, a tattoo, a pin, a phrase on a shirt. Speaks plainly. Doesn't joke much.

**Stats.** +2 Charm / +1 Grit / +1 Cool / 0 Heart / -1 Slick

### Starting Moves

**Witness** (signature) — When you publicly stand on your code in front of someone who has to reckon with it, roll +Charm. On 10+, they reckon — they either join you or move out of your way. On 7–9, they reckon, but you've made an enemy. On 6–, they laugh.

**The Code** — Write down your one principle in one sentence. When you act in line with it at cost to yourself, mark XP. When you violate it, the GM marks one step on your using track *or* wear track (the GM picks which).

**Unflinching** — When you would be intimidated, cowed, or talked down by force of presence, you aren't. The fiction reflects this; you take no -forward from intimidation.

### Advancement Moves

**The Word** — When you make a vow aloud and someone hears it, you cannot break it without consequence. While the vow holds, gain +1 to acts in service of it.

**Smite** — When you publicly name someone as your enemy and they hear it, the bond to them drops to its lowest value. They take -1 forward to act against you for as long as your conviction holds.

**The Acolyte** — Gain a younger character who follows you. They are an NPC, they look up to you, and the GM voices them. They will follow you into trouble.

**Lay Down The Law** — When you publicly call out someone for breaking your code in front of a room, roll +Charm. On 10+, the room sides with you for the scene. On 7–9, half the room sides with you.

### Gear

- The symbol of the principle (cross, pin, tattoo, badge, photograph)
- A book of the principle (scripture, manifesto, journal, dog-eared paperback)
- A vehicle that's a little too clean
- A list of names — people you owe, people who owe you, people who broke the code
- A weapon you've never drawn but carry anyway

### Bond

You tried to save Alex once before any of this started. Describe how, and whether she ever knew you were trying.

### XP Trigger

Mark XP when you stood on the code at cost to yourself.

## The Outsider
*Source: Ranger*

**Pitch.** Lives on the edge of Blueville, half off-grid. Knows the back roads, the marsh, the abandoned subdivisions the hurricanes hollowed out. Social skills atrophied while survival skills sharpened. Comfortable alone in the heat.

**Look.** Tan that goes deep. Wears layers even in summer. Boots, always. Carries less in town than they do out of town. Eyes adjust slowly when they come indoors.

**Stats.** +2 Slick / +1 Grit / +1 Cool / 0 Charm / -1 Heart

### Starting Moves

**The Back Way** (signature) — When you need to move people or product through Blueville unseen, roll +Slick. On 10+, clean route. On 7–9, clean but you owe someone. On 6–, you took a wrong turn and there's somebody you don't want to see.

**Bushcraft** — You can survive overnight in the marsh, the woods, an abandoned property, a hollowed-out subdivision. State your camp; you and anyone with you reduce 1 Wear instead of taking it overnight.

**Pre-empt** — When you spot trouble before others do, roll +Slick. On 10+, the trouble registers to you with about a 30-second delay — warn the party or act first. On 7–9, you have time for one move.

### Advancement Moves

**The Hide** — Establish a place no one else knows about — a shack, a stash spot, a half-flooded house. It can shelter 1–3 people indefinitely until found.

**Talk To Animals (Mostly Dogs)** — You read animals like people. Useful for tracking, avoiding farm dogs, knowing where somebody went. The GM answers your questions about animal presence and direction.

**Stalker** — When you follow someone unseen, you stay unseen until you choose otherwise. Crossing into populated areas may require a roll.

**The Boat** — You have access to a small boat — flatbottom, kayak, whatever. The climate-stressed coastline gets more useful by the year.

### Gear

- Boots, broken in
- A knife you've used on more than food
- A canvas bag with rope, tarp, water, lighter
- A vehicle that handles unpaved (truck, ATV, beater 4x4)
- Something from the marsh — a feather, a tooth, a piece of shell

### Bond

You spent time alone with Alex once, somewhere outside town. Describe the place and what neither of you said.

### XP Trigger

Mark XP when you used your territory to protect or hide someone.

## The Grifter
*Source: Rogue*

**Pitch.** Learned to lift because she had to, then learned to like it. Tells small lies to stay sharp. Played her first long con on a teacher in seventh grade and never quite stopped. The Slick stat embodied.

**Look.** Dresses one notch up from her surroundings — looks like she belongs slightly above whatever room she's in. Reads what people want and reflects it back. Quick smile, quicker exit.

**Stats.** +2 Slick / +1 Charm / +1 Cool / 0 Heart / -1 Grit

### Starting Moves

**Set The Mark** (signature) — When you spend a scene building a false story with someone, roll +Slick. On 10+, they believe it deeply; call on it once later for +1 forward to deceive them. On 7–9, they believe it shallow; one chance to use it. On 6–, they're playing you back.

**Pockets** — When someone underestimates you in a scene, you can lift something small off them or near them without rolling. The GM tells you what's available.

**The Switch** — Once per session, declare that you already swapped one item for another in a previous scene. Establish the swap retroactively; the GM tells you the cost if any.

### Advancement Moves

**Doubletalk** — When you talk to two people in one scene who don't talk to each other, you can tell each a different version. Both believe their version for the scene.

**The Mark Pays Twice** — When you've successfully grifted someone, you can grift them again for half the work — one roll, simpler stakes.

**Disappearing Act** — Once per session, you weren't there. The GM and other players accept this; rewind one beat. Useful but burns trust if overused.

**Reading Hands** — You spot tells. In any social scene, the GM names one thing the target is hiding.

### Gear

- A wallet that isn't yours
- A second wallet that is yours
- Three IDs, at least one real
- A phone with three contact lists
- A jacket with concealed pockets

### Bond

You ran a con with Alex once, or you used Alex without her knowing it. Describe which, and whether she ever found out.

### XP Trigger

Mark XP when you got away with it.

## The Touched
*Source: Sorcerer*

**Pitch.** Hunches that pan out. Dreams that warn. The seer-aunt's grandkid. Unmodded, unexplained, Southern Gothic intuition — the kind everybody in town pretends not to take seriously and then asks you about in private.

**Look.** Carries an absence in their attention; half-listening to something nobody else is hearing. Dresses casually but always wears one specific item — a pendant, a ring, a scarf, a particular jacket — that they don't take off. Eyes that don't quite focus on you the whole time you're talking.

**Stats.** +2 Heart / +1 Charm / +1 Cool / 0 Slick / -1 Grit

### Starting Moves

**The Flash** (signature) — Once per session, declare you saw this coming. Reveal one detail about the current scene that wasn't established. The GM honors it if it doesn't break canon.

**Knack** — Name your specific knack at character creation (always knows when it'll rain, always knows who's pregnant, always knows when something's about to break, always knows when somebody's lying about a specific subject). Once per session, declare the knack triggered.

**The Pull** — When you feel pulled toward something or someone without explanation, follow it. Gain +1 forward on the next move you make in the scene where it pulled you.

### Advancement Moves

**Vision** — Once per session, describe a short scene from a possible future. The GM honors it if it fits the canon trajectory; otherwise the GM says how it gets reshaped before it happens.

**The Cold Spot** — Sense violence before it happens in a place. Name a location and ask the GM if anything bad is going to happen there; the GM tells you yes, no, or "soon."

**Names** — When you say someone's name in a way they hear it, gain +1 forward to all moves toward them for the scene.

**The Other Side** — When you sit with someone who's dying or breaking, you can perceive what's pulling at them — what they regret, who they want to see, what they would say if they could. Useful for last-words moments.

### Gear

- The item you don't take off
- A journal of dreams (not always written in)
- A worn deck of cards or set of stones (the divination tool you pretend not to use)
- A pendant or photo of whichever relative had the gift before you
- A phone with one specific person's number you call when you need grounding

### Bond

You saw something about Alex before it happened — a dream, a feeling, a flash. Describe what you saw, and whether you ever told her.

### XP Trigger

Mark XP when you saw it coming.

## The Reader
*Source: Wizard*

**Pitch.** Knows the system. Legal, financial, technical, bureaucratic — the rules nobody else read. Lawyer's kid who didn't make law school. Paralegal. Tax-prep clerk. The person who reads the fine print and remembers it.

**Look.** Glasses or contacts. Dressed slightly more formally than the room requires. Carries a bag. Reads constantly — phone, tablet, paperwork, even at meals. Speaks in clauses.

**Stats.** +2 Cool / +1 Slick / +1 Heart / 0 Charm / -1 Grit

### Starting Moves

**Read The System** (signature) — When you study a bureaucracy, operation, or organization (Jaw-man's network, the police, a small business, a county office), roll +Cool. On 10+, ask 3 from the list: who has the real power, where's the weak point, what's the unspoken rule, who's about to break it. On 7–9, ask 1.

**The Loophole** — When you propose a legal, financial, or procedural way around a problem, roll +Slick. On 10+, it works and it's clean. On 7–9, it works but somebody notices. On 6–, the workaround backfires.

**The File** — You keep records nobody else has. Once per session, produce a document, screenshot, or printed record that supports a claim you make. The record is real.

### Advancement Moves

**Subpoena** — When you formally demand information from someone embedded in an institution, they either give it to you or admit they're hiding it.

**Precedent** — When you cite a similar past situation that resolved well for someone like you, gain +1 forward to act on it.

**The Connection** — Name someone embedded in a system — a cop, a lawyer, a banker, a clerk, a notary. They take your calls once per session.

**Audit** — When you go through someone's records (legal, financial, digital), you find one thing they didn't want found.

### Gear

- A laptop, well-organized
- A binder or accordion folder of records
- A pen that costs more than it should
- Two phones — personal and the one for clients
- A business card from a job that doesn't quite exist anymore

### Bond

You handled something for Alex's family once — a form, a court date, a tax mess, an insurance claim. Describe what, and what it cost you to handle it.

### XP Trigger

Mark XP when you used the rules against the people who wrote them.
