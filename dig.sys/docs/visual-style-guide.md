# Visual Style Guide — Digital Sissy

This document locks the visual look of Digital Sissy. It pairs with the [[(Digital Sissy) Canonical Continuation Contract]] (story canon) and the [[(Base) Writing Style Guide]] (prose register). Quote freely from this file into individual artist briefs.

---

## 1. Core Aesthetic

**GTA V key art** is the dominant register. Specifically: the loading-screen and promotional-art style associated with Rockstar's house illustration (Stephen Bliss lineage):

- Hard black outlines around characters
- Stylized realism — illustrated, not photoreal; grounded, not cartoon
- Slightly exaggerated proportions in the direction of *iconic silhouette*, not anime exaggeration
- Painterly fill with strong shadow shapes
- Cinematic key-art composition: character isolated against a punchy environment, leading lines, clear read

**Best Friend (2018) by GOBELINS** contributes two specific things only:

- The **eye-drop drug concept** (now canonized as Glitter)
- The **saturated three-color palette** (yellow / blue / red — see Section 2)

It is **not** a character-design reference. Do not borrow its 2D-animation register or its haggard-worn character design.

### The narrative work the look does

The world's signage, advertising, technology, and Glitter visuals are **loud, saturated, hyper-real.** The people inside that world are **grounded, working-class, ordinary** — not glamorous, not cool, not haggard-by-default. This contrast is load-bearing: branded consumption keeps shouting at people who are getting eaten alive by it, but the people themselves look like real Gulf Coast working-class Americans, not exaggerated victims.

When in doubt: **the world is shouting. The people are not.**

## 2. Color Palette

### Primary triad (locked)

| Color | Hex | Semantic role |
|-------|-----|----------------|
| **Yellow** | `#ffff00` | Glitter, signage, advertising, the president's hometown banners, hazard tape, taxi-cab cliché — the color of *consumption announcing itself* |
| **Blue** | `#0088ff` | Glitter's visual side-effect (stars, flashes), neon at night, screens, the digital-self register, the Silverado headlights — the color of *the unreal punching through* |
| **Red** | `#ff4444` | Dealer territory, warning signs, the Silverado bodywork, blood (rare and consequential), neon signage, Jaw-man's brand colors — the color of *appetite and threat* |

These three colors are used aggressively, often saturated to the maximum. **They do not blend politely.** Posters, signage, neon, and Glitter visuals push these to full intensity.

### Supplementary palette (people and world)

| Range | Use |
|-------|-----|
| Heat-bleached pastels (sun-faded yellow, sun-faded teal, salt-bleached gray) | Daytime exteriors, sun-damaged paint, faded signs, climate-stressed coast |
| Muted browns, ochres, dust-grays | People's clothing, untreated wood, gravel lots, working-class textures |
| Deep teal-black to dark navy | Night skies, shadows, interior depths |
| Skin tones | Realistic range, with shadows pulled toward greens or blues to keep people *cool and tired* against the hot primary triad |

### What NOT to do with the palette

- Do not use the primary triad on people's clothing as a dominant color (small accents only — a red baseball cap, a yellow t-shirt logo). The primaries belong to the world, not the bodies.
- Do not soften the primaries. They are pure and bright by design.
- Do not introduce competing accent colors (purple, green-as-primary, etc.). The triad is the triad.
- Do not over-saturate skin tones. People stay grounded; signage screams.

## 3. Character Design

### Register

- **GTA V-style stylized realism.** Hard outlines, painterly fill, grounded proportions with iconic silhouettes.
- **NOT anime.** Faces have realistic proportions; eyes are not enlarged.
- **NOT photoreal.** Illustrated quality, not rendered-from-photo.
- **Working-class grounded.** Characters look like real Gulf Coast people — not glamorous, not exaggerated. Tiredness is a *scene-level* condition rendered through context (a long shift, a bad night), not a baseline character trait. Most characters should look healthy, ordinary, and lived-in rather than chronically worn.
- **Iconic silhouettes.** A viewer should identify each character by silhouette alone.

### Cast quick-reference for portraits

| Character | Distinguishing visuals |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Alex** | 20s, female-presenting but androgynous-leaning (short messy brown hair, slight frame, narrow shoulders), tan skin, light grey eyes, often dressed in clothing slightly too big for her, faded band tees (metalhead from her dad — Metallica-era classic metal), cargo shorts or loose jeans, a dog tag pendant on a cord (from her dad), plausibly mistakable for a teenage boy in profile but clearly female in portrait |
| **Becky** | 20s, **unmistakably feminine — clearly female at any distance.** Bleach-blonde hair (slightly grown-out roots), green eyes, sun-tanned skin. Slim build but **not as thin as Alex** — has some shape. "Valley Girl" aesthetic transplanted to working-class Southern 2050s: long acrylic nails (chipped from real life), unnecessary jewelry (rings, hoops, layered necklaces), heavy mascara, lip gloss, tight cheap fashion-with-effort. Permanent half-smirk, **unbothered** demeanor — the look of someone who spent forty minutes getting ready to look like she didn't try. |
| **Jacob "Jaw-man"** | 30s, polo shirt that fits too well, jagged left-side jaw permanently shifted from the football accident, off-center face, cold eyes, expensive watch, hands scarred at the knuckles |
| **Ted** | 20s, vaguely high-school-familiar in a way that puts you on edge — *recognition without relationship.* Forgettable face you'd swear you know from somewhere |
| **Mom** | 40s, peaked-in-high-school energy, still trying for it, cigarettes, the kind of beauty that's only mostly faded |
| **Mom's boyfriend** | 40s, warehouse-job build, sheepish posture, Glitter-rimmed eyes — *he* can read tired/used; he's the canonical user in the cast |
| **Q (Quentin)** | 60s+, Smoke Shop owner, weathered, soft-eyed, the look of someone who has seen exactly this scene before |

### Portrait composition (GTA key-art convention)

- 3/4 view, character isolated against a simple or branded background
- Strong rim lighting, often from one of the primary triad colors
- Hard outline around the figure
- Subtle environmental detail — never a full scene, just a hint
- Aspect ratio: portrait (typically 3:4 or 4:5)

## 4. Scene Illustrations

### Composition

- **Cinematic GTA-poster framing.** Composed shots, not snapshots. Strong leading lines, strong silhouettes, a clear subject and read.
- **Wide-to-medium framing preferred.** Close-ups should be reserved for high-tension moments where the reader's eye should stop on a face.
- **Aspect ratio:** typically 16:9 or 3:2 (landscape) for scene beats; 4:5 (portrait) for vertical handouts

### Lighting and palette deployment

- Daytime: heat-bleached, hard shadows, the palette appears in signs/cars/cans/tech embedded in the world
- Dusk / golden hour: yellow saturation peaks — the world looks like an old postcard that wants to sell you something
- Night: blue and red dominate, yellow only in interiors and signage. Glitter visual effects (stars, flashes) appear as blue motes in dark spaces
- Interiors: limited light sources, the triad pushed through fluorescent or neon

### Subject-matter notes

- **Glitter on-screen:** when a character is dosed, render the visual side-effect — bright blue stars or sharp light-flashes in their immediate visual field, even in dim/dark interiors. Subtle, not overwhelming. Other characters should not see them.
- **The Silverado:** Jaw-man's. Black or deep red. "Too clean for this part of Blueville." Recurring iconic vehicle.
- **The Smoke Shop:** central hub. Worn exterior, faded yellow signage, blue neon "OPEN" sign, red awning. Interior dim but with strong color pops from product displays.
- **Climate-stress visuals:** rebuilt-too-many-times infrastructure, raised foundations, hurricane scars, sandbags, sea-line creep — present but ambient, not foregrounded

## 5. Location Concepts

### Register

- **GTA V environmental concept art / loading screen art** — illustrated, slightly stylized, atmospheric
- Strong silhouette of the building / space with deliberate palette accents
- **Aspect ratio:** landscape (16:9 or 3:2), or square (1:1) for atlas-style entries

### Locked locations to develop

- **Blueville exterior** — establishing shot of the town. Worn coastal Gulf town, climate-stressed, presidential birthplace banners faded yellow, low buildings, sea visible
- **The Smoke Shop** — central hub. Exterior and interior
- **Perky's** — mom-and-pop diner where Alex worked. Old-timey, red-vinyl-booths, jukebox
- **Wash-N-Go** — gravel-lot car wash. Rusted chain-link fence, cinderblock walls. Recurring meet spot.
- **Alex's apartment** — small, cluttered. The site of the robbery.

## 6. Specific Iconic Elements

- **Glitter vials** — tiny, eye-drop-sized, neon-tinted liquid (blue or amber), clear plastic, packaged in small flat cases. **The "stars" visual** is rendered as bright blue points/flashes in air around the dosed character's POV.
- **Laser sidearms** — accent items, not standard. Compact, matte-finish, with a small visible energy chamber. Treated more like expensive boutique items than military hardware. One or two characters carry them; most use older firearms or knives.
- **The bills** — yellow rubber-banded rolls. Recurring visual.
- **Phones** ("phones" being any communication device in 2050s) — varied form factors: thin glass tablets, ear-worn discreet earpieces, fold-out wrist devices. Alex's is older, slightly cracked.

## 7. What NOT to Look Like

- **Photorealistic.** This is a stylized world.
- **Anime / manga.** Wrong register entirely.
- **Generic Pixar / Disney 3D.** Too clean, too round, too kind.
- **Western comic book.** Too high-contrast-line, too dynamic-pose.
- **Cyberpunk-genre-cliché.** No rain-slick neon megacity. No mohawks. No chrome augmentations. The "not quite cyberpunk" register is essential.
- **Sun-drenched-glamour Florida.** This is a worn Gulf, not a vacation spot.

## 8. Per-Image Generation Specs

- **Output format:** PNG, saved as `{NN}_{description}.png` (e.g. `01_alex_portrait.png`, `01_perkys_exterior.png`). Beat-tied scene images use the `00_BEATNUMBER` convention from the CCC.
- **Resolution:** standard high-quality output from Sora (Sora handles this; no special spec)
- **Variants:** **none required** per request — single best image per brief. (Departure from the TP project's two-variant workflow.)
- **Aspect ratio guidance:** portrait 4:5 or 3:4 for character portraits; landscape 16:9 or 3:2 for scenes and locations
- **File destination:** `Addenda/Digital Sissy/art/` (or subfolders by category — TBD with Ephellon)

This block can be quoted verbatim into individual artist briefs to lock the look:

> **Visual style:** GTA V key art / Rockstar promotional illustration style (Stephen Bliss lineage). Hard black outlines, stylized realism, painterly fill, slightly exaggerated proportions for iconic silhouette, grounded faces with realistic proportions. **NOT anime, NOT photoreal, NOT 3D-pixar, NOT soft watercolor.** Saturated three-color palette: yellow `#ffff00`, blue `#0088ff`, red `#ff4444` — deployed aggressively in signage, technology, advertising, and Glitter visual side-effects, **not** dominant on people's clothing. People are grounded working-class Gulf Coast Americans; the world around them is loud and shouting. Heat-bleached pastel underlying palette for daytime exteriors. Near-future 2050s, climate-stressed coastal Alabama, "not quite cyberpunk." Cinematic key-art framing, strong silhouettes, character-isolated-against-environment composition.
